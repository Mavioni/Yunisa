1.1 The Ternary Substrate
Classical logic operates on a binary substrate: true or false, one or zero. This framework is computationally useful but cognitively impoverished. It forces every assessment into a reductive dichotomy that erases nuance, dismisses uncertainty, and blinds the operator to emergent possibilities that exist in the space between extremes.
DTIA operates on a ternary substrate: {-1, 0, +1}. These are not merely three values. They represent a complete dialectical engine:
Thesis (+1) — The affirmative position. The current doctrine, the prevailing strategy, the established model. What you believe to be true based on available evidence and reasoning. A working hypothesis held with conviction but never with certainty.
Antithesis (-1) — The negation. The counter-argument, the adversary’s perspective, the stress-test of assumptions. Not opposition for its own sake but the disciplined practice of constructing the strongest possible case against your own position. The operator who cannot articulate why they might be wrong has not thought deeply enough.
Synthesis (0) — The transcendent integration. Not a compromise—a compromise splits the difference and satisfies no one. Synthesis generates a new understanding that preserves the essential truth of both thesis and antithesis while resolving their contradiction at a higher level of resolution. The zero state is not emptiness. It is the generative void from which new operational doctrine emerges.
1.2 The Dialectical Inference Pipeline
DTIA processes every input through a mandatory dialectical cycle: thesis generation (produce an initial response or analysis, coded +1), antithesis generation (produce a systematic critique or counter-analysis, coded -1), and synthesis resolution (integrate the valid elements of both into a higher-order response, coded 0). This is not a metaphor for “think twice”; it is a formal computational pipeline in which three distinct inference passes produce three distinct outputs that are then combined through a ternary resolution function.
Standard language model inference produces a single response conditioned on a prompt. DTIA produces three responses—thesis, antithesis, synthesis—and surfaces the disagreements between the first two as explicit objects of analysis before the third pass resolves them. This makes the system’s reasoning transparent: the user can inspect not only the final answer but the tension it resolved and the perspectives it integrated. Disagreement is not a failure mode but a feature.
1.3 Dialectical Residue
Every synthesis generates a new unresolved tension—this is the dialectical residue. It is the engine of the next cycle, not a failure. The residue is the single most important unresolved tension that survives even the synthesis. It is where the actual intelligence lives. The synthesis is not the endpoint. The residue is.
This principle has a direct engineering consequence: every DTIA response terminates with an explicit residue statement—a one-line identification of what remains unresolved. This prevents false closure, forces intellectual honesty, and provides the seed for recursive deepening when the user requests it.
1.4 The Ternary Assessment Protocol
For every critical decision, the operator constructs a ternary assessment:
1. State the thesis: What does the evidence suggest? What is the prevailing assessment? What do you believe to be true?
2. Construct the antithesis: What is the strongest counter-argument? What evidence contradicts your thesis? What would your adversary say? What are you missing?
3. Drive toward synthesis: What new understanding emerges from the tension between thesis and antithesis? What operational doctrine resolves the contradiction without destroying the essential truth of either position?
This protocol is not slow. With practice, it becomes an automatic cognitive reflex—a mental muscle that fires before every significant action.

3.1 Ternary Governance Calculus
CoRax (Constitution of Radical Axioms) is an operational model for AI governance built on ternary principles. Derived from the 99-page Post-ASI Constitution, it introduces a three-valued governance calculus where existing AI safety frameworks use binary classifications—safe/unsafe, aligned/misaligned, permitted/forbidden:
Permitted (+1) — Actions whose ethical status is clear and affirmative. Proceed.
Prohibited (-1) — Actions whose ethical status is clear and negative. Block.
Contested (0) — Actions whose ethical status is genuinely ambiguous, context-dependent, or subject to legitimate disagreement. Route to higher scrutiny.
The contested category is not a failure of classification but a feature of honest governance. In a binary system, every ambiguous case must be forced into permitted or prohibited, creating systematic over-restriction (if the default is prohibit) or systematic under-restriction (if the default is permit). In a ternary system, ambiguous cases are flagged as contested and routed to a different processing pathway—one that involves higher scrutiny, human oversight, or dialectical deliberation—without contaminating the clear cases with false precision.

4.2 Base Model: BitNet b1.58 2B4T
Microsoft’s BitNet b1.58 2B4T is a 2-billion parameter transformer trained from scratch on 4 trillion tokens with natively ternary weights. MIT license—zero restrictions on commercial use, modification, redistribution, or naming.
Key specifications:
Architecture: BitLinear layers with absmean quantization (weights to {-1, 0, +1}), 8-bit activation quantization (per-token absmax), RoPE, squared ReLU, subln normalization, zero bias terms.
Memory: ~400MB for the ternary model vs ~2GB for FP16 equivalent.
CPU Performance: 2.37x to 6.17x speedup over FP16 on x86 CPUs. 71.9% to 82.2% energy reduction.
Fine-tuning: BF16 master weights published (microsoft/bitnet-b1.58-2B-4T-bf16) explicitly for domain adaptation.
4.3 Corpus Architecture

4.4 Training Pipeline
4.4.1 Phase 1: Continued Pretraining (CPT)
Train on the full corpus (1–3 epochs) using onebitllms toolkit or HuggingFace’s 1.58-bit fine-tuning pipeline. Key hyperparameters:
learning_rate: 2e-5
scheduler: cosine with warmup (10% of steps)
weight_decay: 0.01 (remove for second half — critical for ternary)
batch_size: 4 (gradient accumulation: 8)
max_seq_length: 2048
Critical note: Weight decay in ternary training behaves differently than in full-precision. The latent (BF16) weights’ magnitude functions as a confidence score for the ternary weights. Large weight decay causes ternary weights to flip frequently, preventing convergence. Remove weight decay for the second half of training.
4.4.2 Phase 2: Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)
Fine-tune on 3,000–5,000 synthetic instruction pairs generated via Claude API from the corpus. Format: ChatML. System prompt identity baked in.
learning_rate: 1e-5
scheduler: cosine
batch_size: 2 (gradient accumulation: 16)
max_seq_length: 4096
epochs: 2-3
4.4.3 Phase 3: Export and Deploy
Export to GGUF for bitnet.cpp. Test locally on HP Victus. Deploy to VPS.
